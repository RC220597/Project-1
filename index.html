A Brief History of World War 2

World War Two was a major global conflict between 1939 to 1945 and began when Germany, under command of Adolf Hitler, invaded Poland to expand his conquest and the power of the Nazi party across Europe. Following this we saw the rapid conquest of much of Europe by Germany however key turning points created by the Allied countries like Britain, The Soviet Union and USA reversed axis gains in several corners of the World.

The Axis Powers

The Axis Powers of World War 2 were a coalition of nations led by Germany, Italy and Japan which was created due to common interests to expand their territories and asser dominance. Germany under the control of Adolf Hitler focused on Europe, Benito Mussolini led Italy and wanted to create a new 'Roman Empire' and finally Japan under Emperor Hirohito who sought to dominate Asia and The Pacific. They were also joined by other nations such as Hungary, Romania and Bulgaria however were nowhere near dominant in this conflict.

The Allies

The Allies were a coalition made up to oppose the Axis Powers grasp for power throughout the World. The major Allied nations were USA, Britain, The Soviet Union and France. They were also joined by many other countries who together united to defeat the aggressive expansion that had begun to take place. Britain led by Winston Churchill and The Soviet Union led by Joseph Stalin held strong against Germany with The Soviets pushing the German forces away from Russia and Britain fighting the Germans in the Air and reoccupying Europe. Meanwhile USA controlled by Franklin D. Roosevelt played a crucial role in both Europe and the Pacific fighting Japan and finally dropping the first ever Atomic Bombs the World had ever seen wiping out hundreds of thousands and ultimatley ending the War in 1945.

Significant events throughout the war

The Invasion of Poland(1939)

On September 1st, 1939, Nazi Germany invaded Poland sparking the beginning of World War Two. This invasion led Britain and France to declare war on Germany which led to Germany to respond with swift and brutal Blitzkrieg tactics which set the tone for the early phase of the war in Europe.

The Attack on Pearl Harbor(1941)

On December 7th, 1941, Japan launched a surprise attack on the U.S naval base Pearl Harbor in Hawaii. This event brought the United States into the war transforming it in to a truly global conflict. The U.S response led to the Pacific War against Japan, as well as support for the other allies across Europe.

The D-Day Invasion(1944)

On June 6th, 1944, the Allies launched Operation Overlord, more commonly known as D-Day by landing on the beaches of Normandy, France. This massive invasion marked the turning point in the European conflict as it began the liberation of Nazi-Occupied Western Europe and ultimatley led to the fall of Nazi Germany.

The Atomic Bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki(1945)

In August 1945, the United States dropped atomic bombs on the Japanese cities of Hiroshima (August 6th) and Nagasaki (August 9th). These bombings resulted in Japan's unconditional surrender and brought an end to the war in The Pacific. It was the first and only use of nuclear weapons in warfare displaying and highlighting this devestating power.
